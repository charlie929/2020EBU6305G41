Southeast Hill





Southeast hills are located in the southeastern part of China. The vast areas south of the Yangtze River, from the east of the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau to the seashore, are mostly low mountains and hills below 1,000 meters above sea level, collectively called the southeast hills. It mainly includes three parts: Jiangnan hills, Zhejiang-Fujian hills and Guangdong-Guangxi hills.


The topography of the hills in the southeast is characterized by a series of lush and steep mountains, a series of red rock basins and valleys embedded in a series of red rocks, green hills, magnificent mountain colors. Although the vast southeast area is dominated by hills with an altitude of about 500 meters, there are also many low mountains with an altitude of 1,000 meters or more.


In addition to the Nanling Mountains, there are Xuefeng Mountain in western Hunan, Luoxiao Mountain at the junction of Hunan and Jiangxi, Tianmu Mountain in western Zhejiang, Huangshan Mountain in southern Anhui, and Daiyun Mountain and Wuyi Mountain in Fujian. Most of these mountains extend in the northeast-southwest direction, and the mountains are relatively high and majestic.
