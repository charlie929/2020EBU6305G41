Four Plateaus

Chinese four plateaus are concentrated on the first and second terrains, including Inner Mongolia Plateau, Qinghai-Tibet Plateau, Loess Plateau and Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau, with an average sea wave of 800-3000 meters.

Four Basins

China's four major basins include Tarim Basin in the south of Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region, Junggar Basin in the north, Sichuan Basin and Qaidam Basin in the northwest of Qinghai Province.




Three Plains

The three Great Plains are the general name of the Northeast Plain, the North China Plain and the Middle and Lower Yangtze Plain, all of which are located in the third step.




Three Hills

The three hills refer to the hills of Shandong, Liaodong and Southeast, which are the largest and most widely distributed hilly landforms in China.
